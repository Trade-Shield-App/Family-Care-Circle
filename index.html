What the product covers

A family-first support platform built around real life at home.

FamilyCare Circle is not a medical app and not a full care-agency platform. It sits in the middle: reassurance for family, practical support for the older relative, and a clean system for booking help and keeping everyone in the loop.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family dashboard

One shared view of appointments, notes, tasks, check-ins and support history for Mum, Dad or another relative.

🛎️

Book local help

Request shopping runs, prescription pickups, transport, welfare visits, waiting-in and small home-support tasks.

❤

Reassurance check-ins

Simple daily or weekly check-ins with alerts to family if one is missed, plus optional notes on mood or wellbeing.

📅

Appointments & reminders

Track GP visits, hospital appointments, hairdressers, podiatry, prescriptions and family tasks in one calendar.

📝

Live notes & updates

Helpers and relatives can log updates like “prescription delivered”, “sounded tired”, or “engineer completed boiler visit”.

🔐

Trusted circle access

Invite siblings, spouses or neighbours with clear permissions so everyone can help without the chaos of group chats.

How it works

Start simple: one older relative, one family circle, one local support flow.

1

Create the family circle

Add your older relative, invite siblings or family members, and store key contacts like GP, pharmacy and emergency details.

2

Add appointments and routines

Set upcoming appointments, check-in preferences, regular errands and anything the family wants visibility on.

3

Book help when needed

Request practical support in a few clicks: prescription pickups, shopping, transport, waiting-in or a friendly check visit.

4

Get updates back in one place

The family feed shows what was completed, what’s next and whether anything needs attention — without endless phone calls.

Penrith pilot plan

A realistic local-first launch model.

Start in Penrith with a family dashboard and a tightly defined set of support services. Don’t launch as a huge marketplace on day one — begin with a concierge-style helper model and prove demand with a small number of families.

Launch services

  • Prescription collection and drop-off
  • Shopping top-up runs
  • Transport to appointments
  • Waiting in for engineers / deliveries
  • Friendly welfare visits
  • Small household support tasks

Pilot audience

  • Adult children living outside Penrith
  • Local spouses who need backup support
  • Families managing multiple appointments
  • Older adults living alone at home
  • Relatives coordinating from WhatsApp and memory
  • People not yet needing full regulated care
Recommended MVP scope

Version 1 should stay focused.

1

Family accounts + loved one profile

Basic profile, key contacts, notes and permissions for siblings/family members.

2

Appointments, check-ins and reminders

Shared calendar, daily/weekly check-ins and simple alerting if something is missed.

3

Book help + task updates

Structured service request form, helper assignment and completion updates back to the family feed.

4

Admin/helper dashboard

A private operations view for you or local helpers to manage bookings, notes and proof of completion.

Pricing concept

Simple family plans plus support booking fees.

The family pays for the coordination layer. Practical help can be booked as needed on top. This gives you recurring revenue while keeping the product useful even for families who only need occasional support.
